Key Insights

Rice Production Trends

Rice production has shown significant variation across districts. Durg district in Chhattisgarh consistently maintains high production levels, with 2016 being a peak year at 1791.66 (1000 tons).

Wheat Yield Improvements

Wheat yields have improved steadily in most districts, with some districts in Madhya Pradesh showing yields above 4000 Kg/ha in recent years, indicating successful adoption of improved agricultural practices.

Regional Specialization

Certain districts show specialization in specific crops. For example, Raigarh district has significant oilseed production, while Surguja shows higher maize cultivation patterns.

Climate Impact

Year-to-year variations in yield appear to correlate with monsoon patterns, with 2015 showing reduced yields across multiple crops and districts, possibly indicating drought conditions.

About This Project

This agricultural data analysis dashboard was created as part of an internship project to visualize and analyze crop production data across multiple districts and years.

The dataset includes information on various crops including rice, wheat, maize, sorghum, pulses, oilseeds, sugarcane, and cotton. The data spans from 2010 to 2017 and covers districts in Chhattisgarh and Madhya Pradesh.

The analysis focuses on production trends, yield comparisons, and cultivation area patterns to derive insights that can inform agricultural policy and farming practices.
